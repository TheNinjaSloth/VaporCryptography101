Route Transposition Definition

Route Transposition cipher was first created by the Union forces in the American Civil war. Route transposition is another type of transposition cipher. It is named a “Route Transposition” because the encryptor lays out the message in a grid and then chooses a route to encrypt it in.

For route transposition, pick a grid that has at least as many boxes as letters in your message. Pick a place to start your message. Then, going either up, down, left or right, fill in the boxes with your message. When you’re done, fill in any remaining spaces with #’s, or leave them blank. Once this is done, go from left to right, top to bottom, to encode your message. Write it out as one big garbled word. When decoding, simply reverse the process.
