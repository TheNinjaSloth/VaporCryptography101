Caesar Cipher Definition

The Caesar cipher is one of the earliest and simplest ciphers. It is a substitution cipher in which each letter is 'shifted' a certain number of places down the alphabet. For example, with a shift of 1, A would become B, B would become C, and so on. The method is named after Julius Caesar, who used it to communicate military information with his generals.

When encrypting, all you have to do is write the shifted alphabet alongside your regular alphabet, and copy which letters replace which. Given a shift, you can take an encrypted message and decrypt it.
